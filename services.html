Dental Services
We are proud to offer comprehensive dentistry, which includes cosmetic dentistry, dental fillings, dental cleanings, implant placements and restorations, root canals, non-surgical gum infection therapy, extractions, full mouth rehabilitation, dentures, and many more. We are proud to utilize our ivy league education to bring your the highest quality dentistry, while keeping your visits comfortable, affordable, and convenient.
Fillings
Dental fillings are used to repair damage to the structure of teeth. Structural damage can be caused as a result of tooth decay, wear or trauma. After the removal of a problematic tooth structure, the tooth is restored with composite resin (white filling material) or porcelain. Each filling material has its advantages and disadvantages. We will work with you to determine which material is appropriate.
Root Canals
Root canal therapy removes infected pulp tissue within the root chamber of the tooth. The hollowed-out tooth is filled with an antibacterial filling, and the tooth is “capped” with a crown for protection.
Bonding
Bonding is the term used to describe the process of using composite resins to “glue” materials to the surface of a tooth for restorative purposes. Chipped or cracked teeth are typically repaired through bonding. An enamel-like composite material is applied to the surface of a tooth, sculpted into shape, contoured and polished, making the crack or chip invisible.
Crowns
Dental crowns can repair extensively decayed or damaged teeth. Dental crowns can be made of zirconia, gold metals, silver metals, porcelain or a combination of porcelain and metal. In some cases, conservative dental veneers may replace the need for a dental crown.
Inlays, Onlays, and Veneers
Also known as indirect fillings, inlays and onlays made from porcelain or composite materials are a long-lasting yet cosmetic way to provide a “filling” to teeth with tooth decay or similar structural damage. Whereas dental fillings are molded into place within the mouth during a dental visit, inlays and onlays are created in a dental laboratory before being fitted and adhesively bonded into place by your dentist. Whitening
Teeth whitening is perhaps the most commonly recommended cosmetic dentistry procedure. Teeth are often stained from smoking, food, drink (coffee, tea or red wine) or poor oral hygiene. Bleaching the teeth can enhance the appearance of your smile.
Implants
Composite or porcelain laminates that are adhesively bonded to the surface of a tooth to correct and repair chips and cracks and will improve a worn appearance or severe tooth discoloration. Veneers may also be recommended if you have gaps in your teeth or if you have not had success with teeth whitening.
